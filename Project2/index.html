Thoughts on Data Privacy

I try to put the least amount of my info on the web as possible but when it comes to people tracking what posts I like or what pages I follow, I don't really care.

The Times was given access to one such file that included data on over 12 million devices from 2016 to 2017, representing a total of more than 50 billion individually pinged geographic locations. All of these locations can be easily pinpointed on satellite maps, and they include...well, everywhere: celebrity homes, secure government facilities like the Pentagon, and just about every major city or public location you can think of.

I'm a bit on the fence when it comes to location data though. I understand that it could be used for malicious purposes. However, I also am worried that I might lose my phone and leaving location services on allows me to track my phone if that happens. My family also uses it to locate one another if we are out traveling and lost each other. So although it can be dangerous, I also think location services can be helpful in certain situations. But I don't share my location on social media apps like Snapchat since it really isn't necessary for my friends to know where I am at all times.
